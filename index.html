The billion-dollar-class logic

This does not claim instant billion-dollar value. It reflects a large market pattern: underused private space + civic demand + recurring payments + software coordination. The "green carrot" is that the supply already exists, but most people do not classify backyards as shelter infrastructure.

Green-carrot positioning

The unusual part is not the technology. The unusual part is the grouping: backyard supply, temporary housing, welfare/payment routing, municipal reporting, and host automation in one platform.

Why would anyone want this?

Because a city may need quick, low-cost capacity faster than it can build conventional housing.

Why would a host want this?

Because unused land can generate capped, predictable income without becoming a full landlord operation.

Why would this scale?

Because the same software can be configured city by city, with local rules and pilot limits.
